⚛ PAC–μ Spectral Particle Theory

This paper reconstructs the origin of mass, stability, and taxonomy of particles through a compression-based spectral lens. It introduces a μ–modulated Higgs potential landscape and defines spectral stability via topological resonance within the μ–Sheaf framework.

Highlights include:

  • Spectral activation as the generator of particle states
  • μ–Higgs field and energy-level classification
  • Construction of a “Spectral Periodic Table”

This theory provides a new perspective on elementary particles grounded in the structure of expressibility and compressibility.
