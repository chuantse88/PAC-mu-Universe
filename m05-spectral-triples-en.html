M05 – Dirac Spectral Triples and Physical Constants

1. Introduction

The PAC–μ framework adopts Dirac spectral triples as the geometric-algebraic foundation for reconstructing space, matter, and physical constants. Spectral geometry replaces traditional Riemannian geometry by encoding curvature, topology, and interactions into the spectrum of operators.

2. Spectral Triple Structure

A Dirac spectral triple is defined as:

(A, H, D)
  • A: An involutive algebra representing observables
  • H: A Hilbert space on which A acts
  • D: A Dirac operator whose spectrum encodes geometric and dynamic data

3. Physical Interpretation

  • Geometry = spectrum of D
  • Interaction = perturbation of D
  • Mass = eigenvalues of D
  • Distance = inverse of Dirac spectrum spacing

This noncommutative geometric formulation allows space-time, mass, curvature, and even internal symmetries to be unified through spectral data.

4. μ–Compressed Constants

In PAC–μ, physical constants such as:

  • Λ (cosmological constant)

  • G (gravitational constant)
  • mν (neutrino mass)

are modeled as emergent quantities derived from the compressed spectrum of D under the μ-law. This establishes a spectral origin of natural constants through modal geometry.

5. Spectral Flow and Index

μ connects to the index of D:

μ ≈ 1 / Index(D)

This expresses how the topological invariant (Index) determines the scale of compression and thus the physical constants it governs.

7. Summary

Dirac spectral triples encode all physical and geometric features of reality into a spectral framework. Within PAC–μ, μ regulates this spectrum, giving rise to a unified origin of physical laws through compressed modal operators.
