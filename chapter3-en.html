Chapter 3: PAC–μ System Architecture Overview

PAC–μ is a cosmological ontological framework built upon the spectral compression law μ and the six axioms of spectral ontology (SUMS–1 through SUMS–6). Its structural closure arises from the interlinked and inter-nested nature of seven categorical subsystems, forming a full-stack architecture spanning existence, cognition, logic, evolution, consciousness, and engineering.

The system consists of the following seven interconnected subsystems:

  • PAC–μ: Core spectral structure based on SUMS–1 to SUMS–5
  • PAC–Q: Perceptual structure system using sheaf constructions for local cognition
  • PAC–C: Spectral consciousness model defining the Ψ tensor field and language projection
  • PAC–T: Topos internal logic system enabling axiomatized logical capacity
  • PAC–E: Modal civilization transition chain and Moduli space of spectral evolution
  • PAC–G: Geometry-Physics reconstruction system connecting spectral geometry and fundamental constants
  • PAC–A: Spectral engineering system encompassing μ–AI, medical systems, and spectral robotics

3.2 Categorical Coupling

Each subsystem is both a category (with objects and morphisms) and a functorial image within the total system. They interact through:

  • μ–preserving transformations
  • Spectral natural transformations
  • Internal logical embeddings within the Topos

3.3 Global Closure

PAC–μ exhibits structural closure across the following dimensions:

  • Axiomatic closure: SUMS–1 to SUMS–6 provide foundational completeness
  • Category-theoretic closure: A closed loop formed by Sheaf–Topos–Functor–Tensor–Logic

Therefore, PAC–μ is defined as a minimally complete ontological system, applicable to any intelligence, perception modality, or universe structure.
