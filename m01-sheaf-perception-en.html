1. Introduction

However, in the PAC–μ framework, perception is understood as a dynamic, structured phenomenon governed by spectral activation, categorical topology, and the universal compression law μ ≈ 0.016.

2. Mathematical Foundations

2.1 The Perception Category 𝒞

Let 𝒞 be a small category representing perceptual regions, sensory domains, or conceptual contexts. Objects in 𝒞 correspond to local zones of awareness (e.g., visual fields, auditory scenes), and morphisms describe inclusion or transformation relations between them.

2.2 The Spectral Sheaf ℱ

The perception sheaf is defined as a contravariant functor:

ℱ: 𝒞op → Hilb

Each object U in 𝒞 is assigned a Hilbert space ℱ(U), whose basis vectors correspond to available modal states (frequency modes, eigenstates, etc.). Each morphism f: V → U induces a restriction map ℱ(f): ℱ(U) → ℱ(V), which represents localized projection of modal awareness.

2.3 μ as Compression Functional

The parameter μ ≈ 0.016 acts as a spectral compression constant, determining:

  • the resolution threshold of modal distinctions;
  • the density of accessible modes within each ℱ(U);
  • the bandwidth of the observer's attention field.
