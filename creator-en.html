The Origin of PAC–μ Spectral Ontology Framework

Abstract: This paper presents a philosophical and foundational reflection on the logical and epistemological considerations underpinning the creation of the PAC–μ Spectral Ontology Framework. It argues that our present understanding of mathematics and physics clearly indicates an underlying axiomatic structure to the universe, independent of any particular observer or civilization. Furthermore, it suggests that human science and mathematics, deeply influenced by visual perception, are not universally privileged. A hypothetical auditory-based (vibration-centric) civilization would inherently construct a distinct, but equally valid, axiomatic framework. Ultimately, the paper argues that the most fundamental description of the universe must transcend specific sensory modalities, proposing an axiomatic structure grounded directly in quantum states—leading naturally to the PAC–μ framework.
